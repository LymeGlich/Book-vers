Book Description

With over 2 million copies sold worldwide, Strength Training Anatomy has become the definitive resource for strength training enthusiasts and professionals alike. This internationally bestselling book features full-color anatomical illustrations that reveal exactly what happens in the body during exercise.

Frédéric Delavier's visual guide provides a detailed look at how muscles work during strength exercises. The over 600 full-color illustrations show the primary muscles worked along with all the relevant surrounding structures, including bones, ligaments, tendons, and connective tissue. The book explains the exact way each exercise affects your muscles and how adjusting positions can isolate specific muscles to enhance the effectiveness of your workout.

The updated third edition features:

  • Over 100 new illustrations and updated content
  • New exercises for abs, shoulders, arms, and buttocks
  • New coverage of stretching techniques
  • Enhanced explanations of common weight training injuries and how to avoid them
  • Detailed anatomical analysis of rotational movements

This comprehensive guide is an invaluable resource for those looking to increase their knowledge of anatomy and optimize their workouts. Whether you're a fitness professional, physical therapist, or simply someone passionate about understanding how your body works during exercise, Strength Training Anatomy provides the visual detail and practical information you need.

About the Author

Frédéric Delavier

Frédéric Delavier is a renowned French artist, writer, and anatomy expert. A graduate of the prestigious École des Beaux-Arts in Paris, he has combined his artistic talents with an in-depth knowledge of anatomy to create one of the most widely used series of anatomy books for fitness professionals worldwide.

As former editor in chief of the French magazine PowerMag, Delavier is now a journalist for Le Monde du Muscle and a contributor to Men's Health Germany. He is the author of several bestselling strength training guides and has sold over 5 million books worldwide.
